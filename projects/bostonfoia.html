Boston FOIA Tech Stack Redesign

Tech and Innovation in Government with Nick Sinai

Currently in early user research phase, check back soon for more!

Led by former Deputy Chief Technology Officer, Nick Sinai, this field class equips a team of Kennedy School researchers with a methodology and a mindset for driving change in 21st century government. Over the last five years digital services units have emerged as key players in government reform in the U.S. and abroad. The scope and ambition of these efforts vary, but they all leverage methodologies already common to private sector entrepreneurship -- an intense focus on understanding user needs, rapid prototyping and iteration, and the use of modern technologies.

Drawing on approaches pioneered by these units, teams lean startup principles and user-centered design methods to solve real problems for the City of Boston's Public Records department. The project will involve scoping a project/problem in conversation with the client; conducting user research in the field; rapidly designing, building, and testing lightweight prototypes; and, if relevant, create a plan to scale the product. Teams may also offer policy, operations, and strategy recommendations.

For more information on the course, including the spring 2016 and 2017 presentations and blog posts from student teams, please see innovategovernment.org.
